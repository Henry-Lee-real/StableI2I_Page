Evaluating fidelity and consistency in image-to-image generation

Recent image-to-image systems can follow instructions and generate visually appealing results, but they often introduce unintended content drift between the input and output images. Existing evaluations mainly emphasize instruction following, perceptual quality, or aesthetics, while largely overlooking whether the generated image remains faithful to the input in both semantic content and spatial structure. This limitation is especially problematic in real-world I2I applications, where pre–post consistency is critical.

StableI2I is proposed as a fidelity-oriented evaluation framework for image editing and image restoration. Instead of relying on rigid mask-based comparison or only high-level semantic judgment, it jointly considers the input image, output image, and processing instruction to assess whether the intended transformation is achieved while non-target content remains stable. By integrating both semantic-level and pixel-level consistency, StableI2I provides more accurate and interpretable evaluation of content fidelity in real-world I2I systems.

A central motivation of StableI2I is that valid edits may induce reasonable global changes, such as illumination shifts or secondary effects, which cannot be handled well by rigid mask-based evaluation. At the same time, existing MLLM-based evaluators often miss fine-grained structural and pixel-level inconsistencies. StableI2I addresses both issues by dynamically conditioning evaluation on the instruction and explicitly assessing fidelity across complementary dimensions.
