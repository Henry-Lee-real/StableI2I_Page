Overall

A unified framework for evaluating I2I fidelity and consistency

Existing evaluations for image-to-image generation mainly emphasize instruction following and the perceptual quality or aesthetics of the generated results. However, in many real-world I2I scenarios, they fail to assess whether the output image preserves the semantic correspondence and spatial structure of the input image. StableI2I is proposed to address this gap with a unified and dynamic evaluation framework that explicitly measures content fidelity and pre–post consistency without requiring reference images.

StableI2I is designed for a wide range of I2I tasks, including image editing and image restoration. Instead of focusing only on whether an output looks good, it evaluates whether the intended transformation is achieved while the non-target content remains stable. This makes the framework particularly practical for diagnosing unintended changes in real-world systems.

To support systematic evaluation, we further build StableI2I-Bench, a benchmark for testing whether MLLMs can accurately assess fidelity and consistency in I2I outputs. Extensive experiments show that StableI2I provides accurate, fine-grained, and interpretable evaluations, and correlates strongly with human subjective judgments.
